[S1]

Kim et al., PLOS ONE (2023) — Meta-analysis of 22 studies. Microbreaks significantly improve vigor and reduce fatigue, regardless of break type. "Any decoupling activity" works. Autonomy over break content is a key moderator.

[S1b]

Nature Scientific Reports (2024) — Mixed-method study of 308 employees. Microbreaks replenish psychological resources (vigor, fatigue), though the type of break matters. Exposure to nature yielded the strongest recovery.

[S2]

Yadav et al., Int'l Journal of Clinical & Experimental Ophthalmology (2025) — The 20-20-20 rule is a simple and effective strategy for reducing digital eye strain. Outcomes vary by adherence; regular reminders improve compliance.

[S3]

Hansraj, Surgical Technology International (2014) — Forward head posture adds up to 27 kg of additional force on the cervical spine at 60° tilt. Posture breaks directly address this mechanical load.

[S4]

Harvard Health / sedentary behaviour research — Prolonged sitting reduces blood flow, lowers metabolic rate, and increases cardiovascular risk. Standing breaks of even 2 minutes restore peripheral circulation.

[S5]

Jerath et al., Medical Hypotheses (2006) — Slow, controlled breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing cortisol and stabilising heart rate variability. Box breathing 4-4-4-4 is the simplest validated protocol.

[S6]

Kolar et al., Clinical Biomechanics (2012) — Slumped seated posture compresses the diaphragm by up to 30%, reducing tidal volume. Postural correction restores diaphragmatic breathing.

Questions

Is this meditation?

No. The Daily Reset is physical — eye exercises, posture correction, breathing techniques, and movement. There's no guided imagery, no mindfulness instruction, no spiritual component. It's closer to physiotherapy than meditation.

Do I need to download anything?

No. The Daily Reset runs entirely in your browser. On mobile, it works as a web app — you can add it to your home screen if you want, but there's no app store, no update cycle, no storage used.

Is my data private?

Yes. All user data — your schedule, your break history, your preferences — is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server. We don't have your data, so we can't lose it or sell it.

What's Lunch Break Learning?

An optional feature. Pick a topic group — philosophy, motivation, science, or leadership — and each day you get one randomly shuffled 3–5 minute audio episode. One new episode per day, so there's always something to look forward to tomorrow. You can change your topic group anytime, which starts a fresh shuffle. It's off by default; the physical breaks are the core product.

How is this different from Calm or Headspace?

Calm and Headspace are meditation apps — 10–20 minute sessions, mindfulness-focused, broad consumer audience. The Daily Reset is 3–5 minute physical breaks at your desk, designed for working executives, priced lower, and built on occupational health research rather than mindfulness studies.
